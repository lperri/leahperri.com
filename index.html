From Physics to Data Science

I am a trained physicist and practicing data scientist passionate about demystifying complex datasets. Every dataset has a story to tell if you know how to speak its langauge. My training in physics has shaped my ability to think logically, strategically and innovatively. I graduated with a BA in physics & astronomy in 2017 from Northwestern University. At Northwestern, I worked on computational research studying gravitational-wave astronomy. I went on to earn an MS in physics from UCLA in 2018, where I studied particle physics and participated in the Compact Muon Solenoid experiment at CERN. Check out my recent work to learn more about these projects.
